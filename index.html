Safe AI Sweden

A forming community for people in Sweden who want to understand AI safety, AI governance, and responsible advanced AI development.

See how to help Current focus

Current status

  • Volunteer-led and still taking shape.
  • Starting with small learning formats and practical discussions.
  • Focused on Sweden's universities, companies, civil society, and public institutions.

Mission

Build a serious Swedish forum for AI safety and governance.

Safe AI Sweden is an early-stage community initiative, not a research institute, regulator, or policy body. The near-term goal is coordination: help interested people find each other, learn the field, and identify useful ways to contribute.

The Swedish context matters. Universities, companies, public agencies, civil society, and security-relevant sectors are likely to need people who can reason clearly about frontier AI risks, evaluations, deployment choices, and governance.

Current scope: community building, learning, events, and careful cross-sector conversation. We do not currently offer grants, certification, lobbying services, or official policy advice.

Current focus

Practical work that fits an early community.

Introductory learning groups

Small sessions on AI risk, technical safety, evaluations, governance, and responsible deployment.

Swedish ecosystem mapping

Identifying people, courses, labs, companies, policy forums, and local groups relevant to AI safety.

Events with substance

Talks and discussions with clear questions, reading suggestions, and follow-up paths.

Career and project orientation

Helping students and professionals understand skills, projects, and communities worth exploring next.

Cross-sector conversation

Low-hype discussion between technical, policy, public-sector, civil-society, and industry perspectives.

Who should join

For people who want to take the topic seriously.

You do not need to already work in AI safety. The best fit is curiosity paired with intellectual seriousness, willingness to learn, and respect for uncertainty.

  • Students and recent graduates Explore the field, find learning paths, and meet peers in Sweden.
  • Researchers Discuss open questions across computer science, social science, policy, and adjacent fields.
  • Engineers and builders Connect technical work to evaluations, security, reliability, and safer deployment.
  • Policymakers and public-sector people Follow governance discussions and bring institutional context from Sweden and the EU.
  • Professionals from adjacent fields Bring experience from security, law, strategy, education, and operations.
  • Careful newcomers Start with accessible material without needing to adopt a fixed view upfront.
